1. What information we collect

In short: We collect pet information you provide, food bag images you scan, subscription status from Apple, and standard device identifiers needed to run the app.

1.1 Pet profile information

When you set up Kibblefy, you provide information about your pet. This is the core data the app needs to calculate accurate calorie targets. We collect:

  • Pet name
  • Pet photo (optional, stored on your device)
  • Species (dog or cat)
  • Breed or mixed-breed size category
  • Date of birth or age
  • Sex and neuter status
  • Current weight and body condition score (BCS)
  • Activity level
  • Daily feeding logs and portion data
  • Weight history from weekly weigh-ins

1.2 Food bag scans (images)

When you use the Scan feature, you capture an image of a pet food package. The image is sent to our backend server, which forwards it to a third-party AI service (Anthropic) for analysis to extract nutrition information (kcal per cup, brand, product name). See Section 5 for details.

We do not store scanned images on our servers after analysis. The extracted text data (brand name, calorie content) is returned to your device and stored locally.

1.3 Subscription information

Kibblefy is a subscription app. We do not handle payment information ourselves — Apple processes all transactions through the App Store. However, we use a third-party subscription management service (RevenueCat) to track your subscription state. RevenueCat collects:

  • An anonymous user identifier (generated automatically; not linked to your name or email)
  • Subscription status (trial, active, expired, cancelled)
  • Subscription product (monthly or annual plan)
  • Purchase and renewal history
  • Device identifiers needed for entitlement verification

We never see your credit card, Apple ID password, or full Apple ID. Apple's standard payment policies apply.

1.4 Device and application data

To operate the app, we may automatically collect:

  • Device identifiers (anonymous, used to manage subscription entitlements)
  • Device type and operating system version
  • App version and crash diagnostics
  • Camera permission status (only if you grant camera access for the scan feature)
  • Push notification permission status (if push notifications are enabled in a future version)

1.5 Information you provide directly

If you contact us by email, we receive whatever information you include in that message (your email address, name if provided, and the content of your message).

1.6 Sensitive information

We do not collect sensitive personal information such as race, ethnicity, religion, biometric identifiers, precise geolocation, health diagnoses, or government identification numbers.

1.7 Information from third parties

We receive subscription status information from RevenueCat and Apple, as described above. We do not purchase or receive personal information from data brokers, advertising networks, or other third-party sources.

2. How we use information

We process information for the following purposes:

  • To improve the Services. Diagnosing bugs, understanding which features are used, and improving the accuracy of the nutrition engine over time.

We do not use your information for advertising, ad targeting, or sale to third parties.

5. AI and image processing

In short: Our Scan feature uses Anthropic's AI to extract nutrition information from food bag images. The image is processed for analysis and not retained on our servers after the response is returned.

Kibblefy includes a Scan feature that uses artificial intelligence to read pet food packaging. When you take a photo of a food bag:

  1. The image is sent over an encrypted connection to our backend API hosted on Vercel.
  2. Our backend forwards the image to Anthropic, which uses computer vision to extract data such as brand name, product name, calories per cup, calories per piece, and calories per kilogram.
  3. The extracted text-based nutrition data is returned to your device.
  4. The image is not retained on our servers after the analysis completes.
  5. Anthropic's data handling is governed by their privacy policy and our agreement with them. Anthropic does not use submitted images to train their models when accessed through their commercial API.

The scan feature does not analyze your pet, your face, your location, or anything other than the food packaging in the frame.

You must not use the Scan feature in any way that violates Anthropic's terms of service or any applicable law.

6. How long we keep information

In short: Pet data is stored on your device while you use the app. Subscription records are retained for up to 7 years for tax and accounting purposes. Scanned images are not retained after analysis.
  • Pet profile, feeding logs, weight history: Stored locally on your device. Deleted when you remove the app or delete the pet from within the app.
  • Scanned food bag images: Not retained on our servers after analysis. Anthropic's retention is governed by their API terms.
  • Subscription and purchase records: Retained for up to 7 years to comply with tax, accounting, and dispute resolution obligations.
  • Anonymous user identifiers (RevenueCat): Retained as long as your subscription is active, plus up to 7 years for record-keeping.
  • Support emails: Retained for up to 3 years for service quality and follow-up purposes.
  • Server logs (Vercel): Retained for up to 90 days for security and diagnostic purposes.

When we no longer have a legitimate business or legal need to retain your information, we will delete or anonymize it.

14. How to review, update, or delete your data

You can review and update your pet profile and feeding data directly within the Kibblefy app. To delete your data:

  • Delete a pet: Use the pet deletion feature within the app (Profile → Edit pet → Delete).
  • Delete all local data: Uninstall the Kibblefy app from your device.
  • Cancel your subscription: Manage subscriptions in iPhone Settings → Apple ID → Subscriptions.
  • Request deletion of records held by us: Email kibblefy@gmail.com with the subject "Data Deletion Request."

We will process verified deletion requests within the timeframe required by applicable law (typically 30–45 days). Some information may be retained where required by law (such as tax or accounting records).
